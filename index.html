Our Story

Hack and Paint is a distributed game studio. We don’t have an office. Well we do - it’s wherever each one of us feels most happy to work from. It can be your home, it can be a cabin near a mountain lake, it can be an airplane. When we thought about creating the perfect studio, location was always an issue. Do we make a studio based in San Francisco or NY ? Or maybe somewhere where we can get subsidies from the government? What if some great artist or programmer wants to join us but doesn’t want to move with his whole family to a new location? How do we make sure we hire the best people in the world? What we envisioned was a company that doesn’t require people commuting to a specific location every morning and every evening. After all, we are building digital products, we don’t really need to be at the same physical space. Especially not in 2016. This is when the idea of a distributed game studio was born.

We are currently a 12 people team, veterans from the Games, Animation and VFX industries, hailing from companies like Pixar, Google, Blizzard, Blue Sky, MPC, EA Games, Rhythm and Hues, Chaos Group, Ubisoft. Our current team has people in SF, NY, LA, Brighton, Munich and Sofia. This diversity brings unique perspectives to creativity. Living and working where you want is a big part of happiness as well!

Values

Happiness

We believe the happiness of employees should come first because happy employees make awesome products and awesome products make happy users! Hence, we designed the company with employees in mind! A place where, employees will call it their own.

Transparency

Everything in Hack and Paint is super transparent. You want to know something? Just ask. Any financial decisions and information are also visible to everyone. Including sales, equity, and yes - salaries too! We believe transparency is a vital part of making people engaged at work.

Self-management

This is both awesome and super hard to do in practice. It’s much easier when someone tells you exactly what to do, how much time you have to do it and what are the expectations. It’s much harder to KNOW what is the best for the company in any given moment and decide for yourself what steps to take to accomplish that. Using self management practices, we increase our employee capacity to learn and respond to change by empowering more of them to do so. We don’t waste talent and resources needlessly, and we eliminate endless meetings and Gantt chart shuffling, thus resulting in a more agile and nimble organization.

Small independent circles

The reason why so many great projects come from indie studios is because they are made by small teams. When big teams start working on projects together, bureaucracy and politics start to emerge. We are using a system called Holocracy because we wanted to eliminate all those negative artifacts that we have experienced in all big studios. Holacracy removes the usual hierarchy of people and substitutes it with very clear processes and rules. Instead of titles , everyone in the company has clearly defined roles. And those roles can change organically.

Work-life integration

Some companies talk about work-life balance. Some don’t even talk about that. In Hack and Paint we talk about work-life integration. If you are happy and passionate about what you do, why it should be different than life? Work can be part of life! Hence we don’t really have time schedule, vacation restrictions or any type of time management really. Everyone is free to choose how much time they want to spend “working”. Yes, that affects your salary but at the end of the day each person is different and some value their time more than others.

What are we currently working on

We are focusing on 2 things right now - prototyping few game ideas and developing a robust pipeline for distributed teams. Because one of our goals is to be a distributed company, a lot needs to be done in the pipeline that can support that distributed workflow. There are a lot of distributed software companies, but almost zero game, animation or VFX studios. The reasons are mostly technical, but we believe the problems are solvable. We are currently building a platform that we use internally for our own projects and we also give to the rest of the world to use! It is called RGBNotes
